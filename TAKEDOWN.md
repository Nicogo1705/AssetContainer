# Takedown policy

- **Who can report:** anyone — rights holders, users, or security researchers.
- **What:** copyright/license infringement, malicious code, broken integrity (content no longer
  matching the pinned commit/hash), illegal or inappropriate content, impersonation.
- **How:** open a [takedown issue](../../issues/new?template=takedown.yml). For copyright claims,
  link the original work and state your relationship to it; for malicious code, point to the
  file(s) and line(s).
- **Timeline:** reports are triaged on a best-effort basis, normally within **7 days**; credible
  malicious-code reports are handled first and the listing may be pulled preventively.
- **Scope:** a substantiated report removes the **listing only** (the `registry/` entry and its
  index data). The asset's own repository belongs to its author and is never affected.
- **Appeal:** the author can contest a removal by opening an issue, or resubmit a fixed version
  through the normal publication PR.
